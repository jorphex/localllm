## Tweet draft (image 5b)

Benchmarked my Radeon AI PRO R9700 against AMD's published specs with `llama-bench` on a 27B Q6_K model:

- PP vs FP32: 105% (Vulkan) / 77% (HIP)
- PP vs FP16 matrix: only 13% / 10%
- Raw TG vs 640 GB/s bandwidth: 88% / 83%

The FP16 matrix gap is because Q6_K uses quantized/integer paths, not tensor cores. And with MTP speculative decoding, effective TG is even higher than raw bandwidth would suggest.

## Notes

- Measured with `llama-bench` on `Qwen3.6-27B-abliterated-MTP-Q6_K-Huihui.gguf`.
- Model: 27.3B params, 22.4 GB on disk.
- Compute derived as `2 * params * pp_tok/s / 1e12`.
- Bandwidth derived as `model_size_bytes * tg_tok/s / 1e9`.
- Vulkan build was rebuilt with `LLAMA_BUILD_EXAMPLES=ON` to get `llama-bench`; binary at `~/.local/src/llama.cpp/build-vulkan-r9700/bin/llama-bench`.
- Raw numbers: Vulkan PP 917.8 t/s, Vulkan TG 25.0 t/s, HIP PP 676.5 t/s, HIP TG 23.6 t/s.
- Runtime server probe with MTP (same preset) hit ~47-56 effective tok/s on TG, which maps to >1 TB/s of effective weight bandwidth because draft tokens avoid full weight reads.
- Keep this framed as a single-stack measurement, not a universal GPU ranking.
