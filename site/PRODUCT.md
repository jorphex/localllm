# Product

## Register

brand — personal research presence / digital garden

## Users

- The author, as a durable reference for their own model-testing work.
- Technical peers and local-LLM tinkerers who landed here from jjjjj.dev or social posts and want to see what has been tried, what worked, and what didn't on a specific hardware stack and use case.
- Future-self returning to check a decision, a number, or an elimination rationale.

## Product Purpose

A public lab notebook for localllm research. Not a marketing page, not a changelog, not a blog — a place to dump findings from testing and benchmarking so they are findable, readable, and useful to others facing similar decisions. The goal is generosity of detail and clarity of reasoning, not persuasion.

## Brand Personality

Honest, obsessive, methodical, quietly opinionated. The design should feel like a well-kept workshop: every tool in its place, nothing decorative, nothing hiding. It is confident enough to be plain.

## Anti-references

- AI slop: generic SaaS cream/blue, rounded hero cards, gradient text, big metric stat blocks, tiny uppercase eyebrow labels on every section.
- Marketing polish that tries to sell or persuade.
- Hover-lift cards and shadow-heavy depth.
- Side-stripe accent borders.
- Inter or JetBrains Mono as primary typefaces.
- Decorative glassmorphism or over-rounded surfaces.

## Design Principles

1. **Readability under load.** Tables and dense data are the content; contrast, rhythm, and spacing must keep long scans comfortable.
2. **Speed as texture.** The interface should feel fast before the numbers prove it: snappy transitions, immediate feedback, no blocking reveals.
3. **Reactive, not theatrical.** Motion and interaction respond to input and state changes; they don't put on a show.
4. **No default fonts.** The type system must be deliberate and avoid the saturated mono/geometric defaults.
5. **Information first, decoration never.** Every visual choice must earn its place by clarifying data or hierarchy.
6. **Personal, not corporate.** The voice and visuals should feel like one person's workshop, not a startup landing page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast on body text.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; all motion degrades to instant transitions or crossfades.
- No known color-vision-specific requirements beyond contrast.
